Brief Bio

I am currently working in Distributed Data Intensive Systems Lab (Disl) under the supervision with Prof. Ling Liu and pursuing the Ph.D degree in School of Computer Science, College of Computing, Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, GA.

My research interests are in WiFi-GPS Localization, Deep Learning for Localization, Middleware Systems, Wireless Communications Systems, Wireless Sensor Networks and Embedded Systems.

I received my Master degree with the major of Information and Communication Engineering from Beijing University of Technology (BJUT) in 2017. I worked as a research assistant in Modern Communication Technology Lab with Prof. Yanhua Zhang and Prof. Pengbo Si. I also received my Bachelor degree with the major of Electronic and Information Engineering from BJUT in 2014.

Now I lived in Atlanta, GA for a year when I was a teenager, and attended Samuel M. Inman middle school and Henry W. Grady High School. I graduted from Inman with Awards for Achieving Highest Average in Science, Outstanding Achievement in ESOL, CRCT (math & science), and Honor Roll Certificate.

When I'm not working in the lab, you can find me playing tennis and badminton on the court at the Georgia Tech campus.
